David Yang

Former architect turned social entrepreneur and AI-native

I started my career as an architect — buildings where every decision is a systems decision.

A decade ago I walked away from that to co-found Pursuit, a non-profit that has moved thousands of adults from $18K to $90K+ careers in tech. Last year we rebuilt the whole organization around AI: new tools, new workflows, new ways of thinking about how a team produces work at scale.

I don't have the traditional path to becoming an engineer—or even a designer. But I always say that architecture school never taught me how to make buildings. What it taught me was how to zoom in and zoom out, to think both conceptually and at the detail level, to be meticulous but also creative, and to think systematically about design.

'Let's Build': Designing a 400-Person Gala

The Pursuit Bash is our 400-person annual gala — and in 2025, as Pursuit marked its shift to an AI-native organization, the event became a pivotal celebration of that transformation. Instead of hiring an external agency, I served as creative director alongside one designer, bringing the work in-house and using AI to do what would normally require a much larger team: environmental design, collateral, large-format artwork, copy, and production. The result was a fully immersive “Let’s Build” experience — construction-site wayfinding, wheat-pasted portrait galleries, and AI-generated art refined until it felt human, not synthetic.

03

Bash Track: Non-profit Event Management Tool

Bash Track is a full-featured event management platform built solo in 3 months and used to run Pursuit's 2025 Annual Gala. It enables hosts to manage personalized guest lists and track RSVPs in real time, and supports text-to-pledge so guests can donate on the spot. It gives administrators complete visibility into seating, dietary preferences, check-in data, and communications, all in one place. We've since adapted it to run all of our events through it, from Demo Days to staff events.

04

Pursuit Platform: Our AI-powered LMS beta

We run an in-person program, but it's powered by software—an all-in-one learning and career management system that guides participants from onboarding through job placement with AI-powered daily learning, attendance tracking, assessments, and a built-in job search tracker. Staff get real-time dashboards and analytics across every cohort, while builders stay on track with personalized goal-setting, networking tools, and progress visibility throughout the program.

This has been an org-wide initiative and experiment in “Everyone Builds”—where not just the product team but all members of the team actively contribute code to the build. We've cut our SaaS spending in half, and 78% of non-technical staff have contributed code.
